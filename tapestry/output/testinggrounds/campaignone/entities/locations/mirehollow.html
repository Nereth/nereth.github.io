Mirehollow

Location

Description

Mirehollow is a mist-shrouded port city clinging to ancient seawalls and barnacled pilings, its skyline a ragged thicket of stilted warehouses, rope bridges, and slate-roofed tenements. Chill fog coils through salt-slick alleys and along tide-stairs, smearing lanternlight into pale halos while bell buoys toll from the gray water. At low tide, the broken bones of an older civilization thrust up in toppled arches and drowned plazas, basalt vaults yawning beneath the piers where the sea exhales cold air that tastes of brine and old stone. In the fog, the dead sometimes whisper—thin voices threading between moored hulls and storm drains, carrying fragments of names, warnings, and bargains to anyone brave or foolish enough to listen.

The Tide Court holds session in a low, many-arched hall built over a tidal canal, its doors banded in verdigris copper and its foundations set into the ancient masonry below. Warded fog-lanterns burn with steady blue flame along the quay; salt-crusted waystones mark safe paths when the mists come heavy. Kelp-curtained stairwells plunge into submerged arcades, nets dry on creaking racks, and tar and tallow smoke drift on the air, while the constant susurrus of water and rigging gives the city its restless, tidal heartbeat.

Significance

Mirehollow sits at the crossroads of the living and the dead, a strategic harbor governed by the Tide Court and the base of Eris Vanthe’s cult activity. Its whispering fogs and the ancient ruins beneath it make the city a fulcrum for occult mysteries, political intrigue, and seaborne trade, shaping alliances and conflicts that ripple through the campaign.

Events

Mirehollow rises where settlers claim the high bones of a drowned civilization, laying planks and pilings atop cyclopean stone and carving docks into long-forgotten quays. The fog comes early and often, and with it the murmurs of the dead; lanterns and fogbells become fixtures of civic life, as do superstitions about when to speak, when to listen, and when to bar the door. In response to the tide’s dangers—both mundane and spectral—the Tide Court organizes to regulate harbor law, adjudicate disputes, and set curfews and safe routes when the mists roll in.

As trade grows, the Court strengthens its hold, sealing ruin-mouths that belch cold air and rumor, patrolling canal gates, and mapping the underways where smugglers and salvors test their luck. The city’s identity settles into a wary equilibrium: coins change hands on the piers while prayers and oaths to keep the whispers at bay are quietly observed. Yet each season reveals more of the ancient foundations as tides gnaw away the silt, and curiosity draws divers and fortune-seekers into black-water vaults.

Eris Vanthe’s cult takes root in those drowned chambers, timing rites to the turn of the tide and using the whispering fog as oracle and lure. Shrines bloom in forgotten cisterns and beneath abandoned warehouses, and disappearances along the quay stir fear that the dead are no longer content to be voices alone. The Tide Court tightens patrols and convenes public hearings as tensions rise, turning Mirehollow into a contested ground where law, trade, and the occult crash together like surf against the ruins beneath the city.
