Brother Moss

Character

Appearance

Brother Moss is a tall firbolg male with moss‑green fur and gentle, observant eyes. Their broad, soft build carries the quiet strength of old forests, and they move with an unhurried, grounded grace. A staff wrapped in fine silver thread rarely leaves their hand, its glimmering bindings catching the light like dew. Their presence is calm and earthen, as if they carry the hush of rain-soaked moss wherever they go.
They dress in simple, weathered traveling robes in muted greens and browns, often layered with a hooded cloak. Bits of leaf, lichen, and threadbare cords cling to their attire, not as decoration but as the natural consequence of long miles and patient listening at riverbanks and thresholds. Their voice is low and steady, and they often pause to attend to the murmur of water or the sigh of stone before speaking.

Personality

Moss is calm and contemplative, speaking slowly in poetic, sometimes unsettling metaphors that suggest an attunement to patterns others miss. They listen more than they talk, and when they do speak, their words arrive like measured ripples—laconic, dryly humorous, and oddly precise. They value harmony, destiny’s weave, and small acts of care, all of which undergird their role as a supportive healer and steadying presence.
In danger they become decisive and unflinching, favoring open action over subtlety; they have little patience for stealth when time and lives are at stake. Moss interacts with others gently but directly, offering quiet guidance, timely blessings, and a willingness to be the first to step into uncertainty. Their unsettling phrasing never aims to disturb—only to name currents they believe are already moving beneath the surface.

Story Involvement

Drawn by recurrent dreams, Brother Moss traveled to Asterfall just in time to witness the Starfall Convergence go awry as a rift opened above the city. They helped defend civilians from the creatures that spilled through, and their calm under pressure caught the attention of Archsage Lyrien Vaust (recorded once as “Lyrian Vost”). Recruited to investigate the instability behind the rifts and the fraying of fate, Moss agreed without hesitation, claiming the waters had already told them to listen.
At the festival fountain in the Upper District, they first remarked that the water was uneasy, then met the Archsage and learned of the Astral Forge’s destabilization. When rift-born insects attacked, Moss answered with Sacred Flame and Guiding Bolt, and then followed the party below, offering Guidance as they descended toward the lower vault. They helped batter a metal sphere guardian, stood by as the inner vault opened to a spoken password, and quickly healed an ally shocked by a glowing blue crystal. When a shifting-faced intruder struck, Moss’s Sacred Flame helped end it; they secured the calmed, pulsing crystal and then aided in stabilizing the engine with it upstairs.
In the aftermath, they continued the inquiry at the Astral Observatory, again remarking that both sky and water felt wrong. At the Loomspire, Moss paused at the threshold to declare the threads were miswoven, then listened within to the tug and twang of fate’s cords before battle was joined. During the fight, they unraveled a guardian with Sacred Flame. They remained with the party under Vaust’s charge, serving as healer, omen-reader, and unblinking witness to the city’s unraveling—committed to following the threads wherever they led, even into the heart of the Loomspire and beyond.
